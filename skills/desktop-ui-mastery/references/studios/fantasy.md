# Fantasy (Fantasy Interactive) - Cinematic Product Vision

The New York/global product design studio behind interface visions and shipped products for clients across finance, media, automotive, and aviation, plus self-initiated concepts that shaped industry direction. Fantasy's contribution to this skill is not a visual style: it is a method for making software feel like a directed experience, and for using high-fidelity fiction to drag real products toward a stronger future.

## What to internalize

1. **Design the film before the frames.** Fantasy treats a product like a director treats a movie: a narrative arc (arrival, orientation, first success, mastery), pacing, and emotional beats, with individual screens as shots serving the arc. Desktop translation: storyboard the user's first hour and their thousandth hour as sequences before designing any single screen. A screen that is beautiful but wrong for its beat is wrong.
2. **Concept as forcing function.** Fantasy's famous unsolicited concepts work because a vivid, coherent picture of the future creates organizational will that a requirements doc never does. Method to steal: when redesigning an existing product, first design the unconstrained north-star version, then derive the shippable version as a stepped-back plan from it. Designing forward from the current product produces increments; designing backward from a vision produces direction.
3. **Signature moment engineering.** Every Fantasy product has one or two deliberately over-invested moments (a data visualization, an entrance, a transition) that carry the product's entire emotional identity, surrounded by disciplined, quiet utility. Steal the ratio: 95% instrument, 5% theater, with the theater placed at moments of meaning (first load of the user's own data, completion of a milestone), never on the hot path.
4. **Fidelity is an argument.** Fantasy presents motion, sound, and real-data prototypes because feel cannot be evaluated from static comps. Match: judge and present desktop UI as running prototypes with realistic data.
5. **Enterprise deserves cinema too.** Much of Fantasy's best work is dashboards, trading tools, and operational software made to feel premium. The lesson: density and drama are not opposites; a dense instrument can still have an identity, a palette with conviction, and one unforgettable view.

## Signature moves to steal

- **The hero visualization.** One central, custom, live representation of the domain (a network, a portfolio, a fleet) that is both genuinely functional and the product's identity anchor. Not a stock chart: a bespoke view only this product could have.
- **Orchestrated first-run.** The initial experience choreographed as a sequence (staged reveals, data populating with purpose) so the product's value is felt in the first sixty seconds.
- **Narrative case for every design.** Present designs as a story of the user's changing state, not a features tour. If the story is weak, the design is weak.

## Never copy

- Concept-film physics into shipped tools: multi-second transitions, camera moves, and choreography that demo well and infuriate daily users. Fantasy's own shipped work is far calmer than its concept reels; copy the shipped discipline, use the concept energy only for vision artifacts.
- The dark-glossy-glow enterprise aesthetic as a default. It is Fantasy's most-imitated surface and reads as 2019 fintech when applied without their underlying restraint.
