# Mike Matas - Content as the Interface

Designer on the original iPhone software team at Apple (camera, photos, maps, and more), then on the team that designed the Nest Learning Thermostat; co-founder of Push Pop Press (the Our Choice interactive book, acquired by Facebook), a lead designer of Facebook Paper, co-founder of Lobe (acquired by Microsoft), now a designer at LoveFrom. Matas's signature: interfaces where chrome nearly vanishes and the content itself is the manipulable, physical object.

## Core principles, translated to desktop UI

1. **Touch the content, not controls about the content.** In Our Choice, you pinch the photo, tilt the infographic, blow on the windmill. Desktop translation: resize the chart by dragging the chart, edit the value by clicking the value, reorder by dragging the row. Every control panel is an admission the content itself could not be made manipulable.
2. **Continuous, reversible transitions.** Paper's cards expanded under the finger, interruptible at any point, direction reversible mid-gesture. Motion tied to input position rather than fired as a canned clip; on desktop: drag-tracked panel reveals, scroll-linked (not scroll-jacked) continuity.
3. **The chrome-free frame.** Full-bleed content with controls summoned by intent (hover zones, gentle edge affordances). Requires discipline: discoverability must be preserved through motion hints and first-run choreography.
4. **One physical metaphor, fully committed.** Nest is a dial; Paper is cards; Our Choice is a book. Each product commits to a single coherent object-metaphor and derives every interaction from it, instead of mixing metaphors per screen.
5. **Demo-quality is real quality.** Matas's work demos spectacularly because the fluidity is genuine, not staged. If the product cannot be demoed live without a script, the feel is not done.

## Signature moves to steal

- **Direct-value editing.** Numbers, dates, and labels editable in place with scrub, type, or step, killing the edit-dialog round trip.
- **The expand-in-place card.** Item grows into its detail view under the cursor, context dimming but persisting behind, escape or click-out collapsing it home.
- **The committed metaphor test.** Name the product's single physical metaphor in one word; audit every interaction against it.

## Never copy

- Gesture-maximalism from the touch era; hover, right-click, and keyboard are the desktop's native riches, and hidden-gesture UI without them is discoverability debt.
- Facebook Paper's fate is the caution: beloved fluidity, unclear necessity. Feel serves purpose or it is a tech demo.

## Interrogation questions

What is currently edited through a panel that could be edited by touching the thing itself? What single metaphor governs this product? Could this design be demoed live, unscripted, today?
