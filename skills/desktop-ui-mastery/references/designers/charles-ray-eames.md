# Charles and Ray Eames - Details, Constraints, and the Good Host

The husband-wife office behind the molded chairs, the Eames House, IBM exhibitions, and the film Powers of Ten. Their transferable doctrine: design is a plan for arranging elements to accomplish a purpose; the details are not the details, they make the design; and the designer's role is that of a good, thoughtful host anticipating the needs of guests.

## Core principles, translated to desktop UI

1. **The good host.** Anticipate needs before they are voiced: the file dialog already in the right folder, the paste that matches formatting intent, the default that reflects the last choice. Hospitality is the warmest available framing for smart defaults and context inference.
2. **The details make the design.** The Eames chairs succeed in the shock mounts and edge radii. Quality lives in the invisible connective tissue: focus order, error copy, the gap between panels. There is no polish phase; there is only design.
3. **Constraints are the brief's gift.** Their best work came from material and cost limits embraced fully. Treat platform limits, density requirements, and performance budgets as form-givers, not obstacles.
4. **Serious play.** The office prototyped through toys, films, and experiments; play was their research method. Build throwaway interactive prototypes to discover behavior that static thinking cannot.
5. **Powers of Ten thinking.** Their famous film moves smoothly across scales. Interfaces should too: overview to detail as one continuous, orientation-preserving movement (zoom, expand-in-place) rather than a teleport between disconnected screens.

## Signature moves to steal

- **The scale-continuum navigation.** Dashboard number expands into its trend, into its records, into one record, each transition preserving context.
- **Anticipatory defaults.** Every input pre-filled with the most probable value, always correctable.
- **Prototype the connection.** When two parts of the UI must join (panel and canvas, list and detail), prototype the joint itself, the way Eames prototyped chair mounts.

## Never copy

- Mid-century furniture aesthetics as UI skin (plywood textures, atomic-era motifs).
- Playfulness in the product when it belongs in the process. Their films were playful; their chairs are disciplined.

## Interrogation questions

Where would a thoughtful host have already acted? Which joint between components deserves prototype attention? Does moving between overview and detail preserve orientation?
