# Benji Taylor - Warm Precision

Founder of Los Feliz Engineering, the studio behind Family, the self-custody wallet whose interface details (recovery flows, transaction sheets, onboarding) set a widely cited bar for consumer-crypto craft, and co-author of the Family Values design writings; after Aave Labs acquired the studio in 2023 he served as Aave's CPO, then led design at Base, and is now Head of Design at X. Taylor's lens: bringing warmth, narrative, and obsessive detail to a domain (self-custody finance) defined by fear, proving that friendliness and rigor multiply rather than trade off.

## Core principles, translated to desktop UI

1. **Design for the scariest moment first.** Family's defining work is the seed-phrase and recovery experience: the moment users are most afraid of irreversible loss. Identify your product's equivalent (deleting data, moving money, granting access) and spend disproportionate craft there; trust earned at the scary moment halos the whole product.
2. **Warmth is engineered, not decorated.** Family feels friendly through behavior: forgiving flows, humane copy, celebratory-but-brief confirmations, interfaces that assume good faith. Warmth from behavior survives; warmth from mascots and pastel gradients curdles.
3. **Details are the brand.** The team's public identity is built on interaction details others skip: the way a sheet stacks, a number rolls, a card flips. In commodity categories, accumulated micro-craft is the only unforgeable differentiator.
4. **Design-engineering as one craft.** Family Values describes designers who build and engineers who design, prototyping in production code. The organizational lesson: feel-critical products cannot survive a handoff wall.
5. **Progressive trust disclosure.** Complexity (fees, addresses, signatures) revealed in layers matched to user readiness, with the full truth always one tap deeper, never hidden. Simplification that conceals consequential facts is disqualifying in financial UI.

## Signature moves to steal

- **The ceremony gradient.** Interaction weight scaled to consequence: instant for browsing, one confirmation for sending, deliberate multi-step ceremony with comprehension checks for irreversible acts.
- **Numbers with body.** Balances and values that roll, count, and settle with physicality, making abstract figures feel owned.
- **Recovery as a designed product.** Backup, restore, and failure paths given first-class design attention and their own success metrics.

## Never copy

- The Family look (its cards, springs, and sheet choreography) which is among the most screenshot-copied surfaces in modern product design.
- Consumer-crypto maximal smoothness in institutional contexts where auditability and density outrank charm; steal the trust architecture, retune the register.

## Interrogation questions

What is this product's scariest moment, and does it receive the most craft? Is the warmth here behavioral or cosmetic? Does every simplification keep the full truth one step away?
